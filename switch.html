Change of scenery

I have seen this coming even before I joined my first ship. I have been curious with how computer softwares/programs are made ever since I played my first computer game. I buried that curiosity when I decided to join a maritime school. As I continued my studies, there was this one practical advice from my college instructors whom are fellow seafarers that stuck with me:
"Our profession is a high-risk and short-lived one - one foot in the grave. It lasts only for as long as we are mentally, physically and psychologically healthy."

I started my career at sea on September of 2013. The first time is always tbe hardest as they say. Learning to adopt to my new environment has been challenging. More importantly, I find the experience valuable, especially in improving my self-discipline and be more mature. Being isolated far away from everyone will give you a different perspective in life.

And then, pandemic happened. Lockdown was implemented and was stuck at home with an unfamiliar situation. Prior to that, my life revolved mainly on - joining a ship, finishing my contract, going home for vacation. Now with more free time available, I decided to indulge myself with learning other stuffs not maritime related. My curiosity with programming steamrolled into a passion from which I decided that I want to take on this new profession. A new career. A change of scenery. As Albert Einstein once quoted:
"In the middle of adversity there is great opportunity."
